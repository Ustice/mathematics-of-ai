# Lesson 34 - Partial Derivatives

## What Clicked

- A partial derivative was consistently understood as varying one input while holding the others fixed.
- Polynomial, trigonometric, and chain-rule partial derivatives were computed accurately.
- Coordinate sensitivities were combined correctly in a first-order total-change estimate.
- The need for all partial derivatives, rather than one component, was connected to the steepest direction.

## Refinements To Carry Forward

- State that the scalar field increases or decreases in a coordinate direction; avoid saying that the partial derivative itself is increasing when only its sign has been evaluated.
- In a Lagrangian, a term such as $\lambda x$ differentiates to $\lambda$ with respect to $x$ when $\lambda$ is held fixed.
- Stationarity equations identify candidates; they do not alone prove a local minimum or maximum.
- A centered finite difference evaluates the function at perturbations of the target point on both sides.

## Readiness

The work demonstrates readiness for Gradients. The remaining issues are local algebra and implementation precision, not conceptual blockers.
